## The Rules

1. **Nothing over £2 per serving.** If a recipe costs more, it's not in this zine.
2. **No weird ingredients.** If you can't find it at a corner shop, forget it.
3. **Minimal equipment.** One pot, one pan, one knife. Maybe a baking tray.
4. **Feeds at least two.** Cooking for one is inefficient and sad.

## Pantry Staples

Keep these stocked and you can always eat:

- Tinned tomatoes
- Onions
- Garlic
- Eggs

> If you have rice and an egg, you have dinner. Everything else is a bonus.

## Egg Fried Rice

The ultimate broke meal. Ten minutes, one pan, infinite variations.

**You need:**

- 2 cups leftover rice (cold, ideally day-old)
- 2 eggs
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp oil
- Whatever veg you have (frozen peas, spring onion, carrot)

**Do this:**

1. Heat oil in a large pan, screaming hot
2. Add veg, stir-fry 2 minutes
3. Push veg to the side, crack in eggs
4. Scramble the eggs, then mix with veg
5. Add rice, break up any clumps
6. Pour soy sauce around the edge of the pan (not on the rice)
7. Toss everything together for 2 minutes

/column

**Tips:**

- Cold rice is essential. Hot rice goes mushy.
- The soy sauce hitting the hot pan is where the flavour comes from.
- Add a splash of sesame oil at the end if you're feeling fancy.
- Leftover chicken, ham, or prawns all work.

**Cost:** About 60p per serving.

## Garlic Pasta

When you literally have nothing else.

**You need:**

- 200g spaghetti
- 4 cloves garlic, sliced thin
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- Chilli flakes
- Salt

**Do this:**

1. Cook pasta in salted water
2. Meanwhile, gently fry garlic in oil until golden (not brown!)
3. Add chilli flakes, cook 30 seconds
4. Drain pasta, save a cup of water
5. Toss pasta in the garlic oil with a splash of pasta water

**Cost:** About 30p per serving.

## One-Pot Dal

Cheap, warming, and gets better the next day.

**You need:**

- 200g red lentils
- 1 tin chopped tomatoes
- 1 onion, diced
- 2 cloves garlic
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 600ml water
- Salt

**Do this:**

1. Fry onion and garlic until soft
2. Add cumin and turmeric, stir 1 minute
3. Add lentils, tomatoes, and water
4. Bring to the boil, then simmer 25 minutes
5. Stir occasionally --- it thickens as it cooks
6. Season with salt

Serve over rice with a squeeze of lemon if you have one.

**Cost:** About 45p per serving.

## Bean Chilli

Hearty, freezes well, works with rice, bread, or chips.

**You need:**

- 1 tin kidney beans
- 1 tin chopped tomatoes
- 1 onion, diced
- 2 cloves garlic
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- Chilli powder to taste

**Do this:**

1. Fry onion and garlic until soft
2. Add spices, stir 1 minute
3. Add tomatoes and drained beans
4. Simmer 20 minutes, lid off
5. Season well

**Upgrades:** Add a grated carrot. A square of dark chocolate. A tin of sweetcorn. Sour cream on top.

**Cost:** About 55p per serving.

## Potato Soup

Five ingredients. Zero skill required.

**You need:**

- 4 medium potatoes, cubed
- 1 onion, diced
- 1 litre stock (a cube is fine)
- Butter or oil
- Salt, pepper

**Do this:**

1. Fry onion in butter until soft
2. Add potatoes and stock
3. Boil, then simmer 20 minutes until potatoes are falling apart
4. Mash roughly in the pot (leave some chunks)
5. Season generously

**Upgrades:** Stir in grated cheese. Top with fried bacon bits. Add a tin of sweetcorn.

**Cost:** About 35p per serving.

## Quesadillas

When you want something that feels like takeaway.

**You need:**

- Tortilla wraps
- Grated cheese (any kind)
- Tinned beans (black or kidney)
- Whatever salsa or hot sauce you have

**Do this:**

1. Lay tortilla in a dry pan on medium heat
2. Cover half with cheese and beans
3. Fold in half
4. Cook 2 minutes each side until golden and crispy
5. Cut into wedges

**Tips:** Add leftover rice, chicken, or veg. Dip in sour cream or hot sauce.

**Cost:** About 50p per serving.

## Emergency Meals

For when you really, truly have nothing:

**Toast variations:**

- Beans on toast (the classic)
- Egg on toast (upgrade: fried in butter)
- Cheese on toast (grill until bubbly)
- Peanut butter + banana on toast

**Packet noodle upgrades:**

- Crack an egg in while cooking
- Add frozen peas in the last 2 minutes
- Stir in a spoonful of peanut butter
- Top with a fried egg and chilli sauce

/column

## Shopping Tips

- **Buy own-brand everything.** The difference is packaging, not food.
- **Frozen veg is fine.** Often better than "fresh" that's been sitting there a week.
- **Check the reduced section** every time. Freeze what you find.
- **Buy spices from Asian or African grocers.** A fraction of supermarket prices.
- **Rice from the big bag** is always cheaper per kilo.
- **Eggs are the best protein per pound.**
