## Before You Start

Urban foraging is the practice of gathering wild food from public spaces --- parks, verges, abandoned lots, riverbanks. It's free, it's seasonal, and it connects you to where you live in a way grocery shopping never will.

### Ground Rules

1. **Identify with certainty** --- if you're not 100% sure, don't eat it
2. **Avoid roadsides** --- heavy metals and pesticide drift
3. **Leave plenty** --- take less than a third of what's there
4. **Check local laws** --- some parks prohibit foraging
5. **Wash everything** --- dogs exist

/column

### What You'll Need

- A field guide (this one, plus a proper botanical one)
- Paper bags (not plastic --- things sweat and rot)
- A small knife or scissors
- A basket or tote bag

### Seasons at a Glance

| Season | Look for |
|--------|----------|
| Spring | Nettles, wild garlic, dandelion greens |
| Summer | Elderflower, berries, chamomile |
| Autumn | Blackberries, rosehips, mushrooms |
| Winter | Chestnuts, pine needles, winter cress |
